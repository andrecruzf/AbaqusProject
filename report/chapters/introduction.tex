\chapter{Introduction}\label{chap:introduction}

\section{Motivation}

Sheet metal forming is a fundamental manufacturing process in the automotive, aerospace, and consumer goods industries. A key challenge in forming operations is predicting the onset of localised necking and fracture, which limits the deformation that a blank can undergo before failure. The \textit{Forming Limit Diagram} (FLD) is the standard tool used to characterise this limit: it maps, in principal strain space $(\varepsilon_2, \varepsilon_1)$, the boundary between safe forming and failure.

Experimental determination of FLDs requires significant material and time resources. Numerical simulation via Finite Element Analysis (FEA) offers a complementary approach: once a calibrated material model is available, FLDs can be computed efficiently for a range of conditions (thickness, friction, strain rate) without additional physical tests.

\section{Objectives}

The objective of this semester project is to build a complete, automated FEA pipeline for the numerical generation of Forming Limit Curves (FLCs) using the Nakazima test. Specifically, the goals are:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Implement the Nakazima test geometry in Abaqus/Explicit with a VUMAT user material subroutine based on the Hosford-Coulomb (HC) failure criterion.
  \item Simulate a range of specimen widths (W20 to W200\,mm) to cover the full strain path spectrum from uniaxial tension to equibiaxial stretching.
  \item Automate the pipeline from model building to post-processing (strain extraction and FLC plotting) on the ETH Euler HPC cluster.
  \item Extract the critical principal strains at failure and assemble the numerical FLC.
\end{enumerate}
